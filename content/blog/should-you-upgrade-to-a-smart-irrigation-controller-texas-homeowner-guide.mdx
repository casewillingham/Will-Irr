If your sprinkler system still runs on a basic timer, you could be wasting water—and money—without even realizing it.

Smart irrigation controllers are becoming more common across North Texas, but are they actually worth it?

Here’s a straight answer:

## What Is a Smart Irrigation Controller?

A smart controller connects to Wi-Fi and automatically adjusts your watering schedule based on:

Local weather conditions

Rainfall

Temperature

Seasonal changes

Instead of running on a fixed schedule, your system adapts in real time.

## 🚨 The Problem with Traditional Controllers

Most older controllers are set once… and forgotten.

That leads to:

Watering during rain

Overwatering in cooler months

Underwatering during heat waves

Higher water bills

In places like Fort Worth, where weather can change fast, this becomes a real issue.

## 💧 How Smart Controllers Save Water (and Money)

Smart controllers eliminate guesswork.

They can:

✔ Skip watering when it rains

✔ Adjust run times automatically

✔ Prevent runoff and pooling

✔ Optimize watering for plant type

Many homeowners see noticeable savings on their water bill—especially during peak summer months.

## 📱 Control Your System From Your Phone

One of the biggest advantages:

You can control your entire system from anywhere.

Turn zones on/off remotely

Adjust schedules instantly

Get alerts for system issues

Monitor water usage

This is especially helpful if:

👉 You travel

👉 You manage multiple properties

👉 You just don’t want to mess with the controller box

## 🔧 When It Makes Sense to Upgrade

A smart controller is a great investment if:

Your current controller is outdated or failing

You want to reduce your water bill

You’re tired of manually adjusting schedules

You want more control and visibility

## 💰 What Does It Cost?

In most cases, upgrading to a smart controller is one of the more cost-effective improvements you can make to your irrigation system.

Compared to ongoing water waste, it often pays for itself over time.
