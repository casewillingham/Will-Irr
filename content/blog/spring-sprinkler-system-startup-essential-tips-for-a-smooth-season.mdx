As winter fades and the warmer months approach, it’s time to get your sprinkler system ready for the growing season. A proper spring startup ensures your irrigation system runs efficiently, preventing costly repairs and wasted water. Here are expert tips from Willingham Irrigation to help you get your system up and running smoothly.

1. Turn On the Water Supply and Check the Backflow

The first step in a spring startup is slowly turning on the main water valve to allow water to flow into the system gradually. This helps prevent a sudden pressure surge that could cause damage.

Once the system is pressurized, check the backflow preventer for leaks or damage. This component is crucial for keeping your irrigation water separate from your drinking water supply.

2. Test Each Zone One at a Time

Run each zone manually and check for:

✅ Leaks or pooling water around sprinkler heads

✅ Uneven spray patterns

✅ Dry spots or overspray on sidewalks and driveways

Make adjustments as needed to ensure proper coverage and efficient water use. If a sprinkler head is not spraying correctly or is misaligned, it may need repositioning or replacement.

3. Program Your Controller for Seasonal Needs

Update your sprinkler schedule to match spring watering recommendations:

🌱 Early mornings are best for watering (before 10 AM)

🌦️ Adjust for rainfall – don’t overwater!

🔋 Replace controller backup batteries if needed

If you’re using a smart irrigation controller, check the settings to ensure it’s properly adjusting for weather conditions.
